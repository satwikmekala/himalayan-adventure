Before You Arrive

"The mountains don't grade on effort. Show up ready or show up humbled."

This trip is the reward. For the next 36 days, earn it.
Push harder at work so you leave with zero guilt.
Push harder in the gym so your legs don't quit on Day 1 of the trek.
Show up to these mountains having actually prepared.

🏃‍♂️ Start this week

Run

3km → 8km over the month

Altitude will cut your lung capacity by 30%. Start building now. Run 4x a week. Increase 1km every 5 days.

🏋️ Critical for trek

Gym: Legs

3 sessions per week, legs priority

Squats, lunges, step-ups, calf raises, Bulgarian split squats. Your quads take 80% of the load on descent. They need to be ready.

🏀 Do this with the group

Sports

At least 2x per week

Badminton, football, basketball — anything with lateral movement and varied cardio. Gets you fit AND keeps it fun. Organise games.

🪜 Best trek prep

Stairs

30 mins, 3x per week

Best simulation for the trek. Find a building and use it. Add a weighted backpack in week 3. Your knees will thank you.

By June 12, you should be able to walk 6–7 hours with a 15kg pack.

The trek will strip everything back. There's nowhere to perform at 4,300m.
Come in with space — not a head full of unfinished business and digital noise.
The work you do mentally in the next 36 days will shape what the mountains give you.

Meditate

10 minutes a day is enough to change everything.

Use Headspace, Waking Up, or just sit in silence for 10 minutes. No goal. No outcome. Just practice being still. By the time you're at Brighu Lake, you'll understand why this mattered.

Apps: Headspace · Waking Up · Insight Timer

Journal

Write before you go. The mountains will respond.

Morning pages or evening reflection — doesn't matter. One question to start: What do I want from 9 days in the mountains with my closest people? Write without editing yourself.

Even 5 minutes. Even voice memos. Just reflect.

Close Your Loops

Finish what needs finishing. Come in clean.

Work, conversations, decisions you've been putting off. The guilt of unfinished things follows you into the mountains. June 12 is the cutoff. Everything before that line gets resolved.

Start your wind-down 5 days before. Don't leave mid-chaos.
"What version of yourself do you want to leave in the mountains?
What are you carrying right now that this trip could help you put down?"

These take time. Trekking boots need 1 month minimum to break in. Buy them this week or pay for it with blisters on Day 5.

🥾 Trekking boots
Break in immediately. Wear them everywhere. New boots on a 3-day trek = destroyed feet.
Buy this week
🧥 Puffer / down jacket
Brighu Lake camps hit -2 to 5°C at night. This is not optional.
Buy or borrow now
👕 Moisture-wicking trekking shirts × 3
Cotton kills on a trek — holds sweat, adds weight, causes chafing.
🩲 Thermal base layers (top + bottom)
Non-negotiable for the summit night. Lightweight thermals.
🧦 Wool hiking socks × 5 pairs
No cotton. Ever. Merino wool only. Your feet will thank you.
🕶️ UV sunglasses
Altitude sun at 4,300m is brutal. UV damage at high altitude is real.
Important
☀️ Sunscreen SPF 50+ (2 bottles)
Burns happen fast. Pack more than you think you need.
🔦 Headlamp + spare batteries
Non-negotiable. Early morning starts and camp nights.
💧 1L+ water bottle (Nalgene or similar)
Hydration at altitude is the single biggest factor in how you feel.
🔋 Power bank 20,000mAh minimum
No charging on the 3-day trek. Camera, phone, headlamp — all on you.

Don't haul this from Hyderabad. Manali has everything and rental shops are solid.
Book or confirm with your trek guide in advance.
